## How to read this map

Keywords are grouped into **clusters that share one target page** — Google
ranks pages, not keyword strings, and splitting near-identical variants
("PR agency Libya" vs "public relations agency Libya") across separate pages
would cannibalize. Priority reflects: business value × realistic difficulty ×
how underserved the SERP currently is.

**Key market findings (July 2026):**

1. **English PR terms for Libya are wide open.** SERPs are dominated by
   international directories (Sortlist, Clutch, TechBehemoths, TopSEOs) and
   results that bleed to **Tripoli, Lebanon**. Only one strong local
   competitor with a real PR positioning (LEGACY, legacy.ly). A dedicated,
   well-structured Pattrix page can realistically rank.
2. **"Tripoli" is ambiguous** (Lebanon vs Libya). Every page must say
   "Tripoli, Libya" in title/H1/copy, and structured data already anchors
   the LY address — this is a real ranking differentiator.
3. **Arabic SERPs are served by digital-marketing agencies** (Artyzine,
   Vomotions, Tabadull, Advertema) and Gulf agencies ranking loosely for
   generic terms. Almost nobody owns Arabic **strategic communications /
   علاقات عامة** content specific to Libya. Arabic is the biggest gap and
   likely the larger search volume locally.
4. Current Pattrix site is a one-page experience + case studies: **no
   dedicated service pages exist yet**, so every cluster below currently
   maps to the homepage or a `/#services` anchor at best.

Competitor type legend: `DIR` directories/listicles · `LOCAL-DM` local
digital-marketing agencies · `LOCAL-PR` local PR/comms agency ·
`GULF` Gulf/regional agencies · `PROD` production houses · `WEAK` thin or
mismatched results.
